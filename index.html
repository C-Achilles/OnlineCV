Curriculum Building

I believe that programming and robotics should be accessible and fun at all levels. While teaching the current robotics unit to a Year 8 class last year, I noticed most students had difficulty with the number of programmable components and how they interact with each other, resulting in very few successful deployments of remote-controlled cars. As a response to this, I redeveloped the lesson to better support students with a scaffolded resource, which resulted in increased student engagement, a calm environment to work in, and greater success in functioning robots, giving them a real-world result of their efforts.

The department has recently bought new robotics that I hope to integrate into the curriculum to make complex tasks more inclusive while encouraging computational thinking. As Head of Department, I intend to further utilise these new technologies to develop greater schemes of work that will increase student attainment and foster a love of learning.

Along with this, I would look to embed digital literacy thoroughly throughout KS3 as we still take on GCSE and sixth form students that seem to be lacking computer skills that are necessary for their education and life beyond school. This includes, but is not limited to, the ability to take screenshots, create well-named file paths, and understanding how to export files into different file types.
